About Us

We have a population of around 1.2 billion and it is just a matter of time, that we will be the nation with the most number of young and technically sound work forces. Technology has grown in many forms and can be made accessible almost to all. It is just a luxury for some but can ease the life of many. It can be the key to solving some of the most pressing issues faced by the country today. We just need some ideas and tinkering to apply it on the field.

An example, detecting and diagnosing diseases is a very time-consuming and expensive affair, almost unavailable in remote areas. An interesting fact - Today, smartphones have a better reach than basic healthcare facilities! Last year a student team developed a low-cost smartphone operated microscope, to detect malaria, though not accurately but the results were convincing enough to improve upon and take it further.

It is only by solving such basic problems that development will occur collectively in every community and not selectively. Dhiti(sanskrit for “An idea”) is a platform for passionate individuals who ideate and aspire that technology reaches the grassroots. Here we will provide you with problems and mentors to form feasible solutions to various issues and provide recognition to your solution. So , let’s put our ideas and knowledge bases to some real use.

Abstract Submission

Participants can either Take up any one problem statement from those given under different specific areas and suggest a technically and financially sound solution. (From Given Problem Pool)

Mentorship

The Mentorship phase will kick off as soon as the results of the Abstract Phase are declared. Shortlisted teams will be given an option of mentorship.

We consider mentorship essential for the participants so as to give them an insight of the exact problems that are faced at the grassroots, of the situations and the constraints that may be involved during the implementation of these solutions. Mentorship will be provided in the following two forms:

  • Technical assistance to the participants (without changing the basic idea of the solution), if required.
  • Assistance in getting a clearer idea regarding the problem statement which will help the participants to arrive at a more suitable and feasible solution to the problem.

Assistance will be provided by the professors of BITS and selected NGOs. The allotment of mentors will be based on the category and appropriateness of the solution.

Evaluation during Mentorship phase:

  • The teams will be evaluated continuously during the mentorship phase.
  • They are expected to submit an intermediate report, and a final report, showing their progress in the project.
  • At the end of this phase, there will be a telephonic conference interview with the shortlisted participants. They will be interviewed by a panel consisting of a professor and a student related to the particular technical field.
  • The participants proceeding to the final round will be decided based on the weekly reports, feedback and the interview. The decision of the judges will be final and binding.

Mentorship Phase ends on February 4th, 2015. The teams are required to submit the final reports by February 5th, 2015.

After this round, 10 teams will be selected for the final round which will be conducted during the technical festival APOGEE 2015.

Presentation

  • The selected teams will have to submit a hardcopy of their report, and give a final presentation on their solutions in front of our Judges, during our event in APOGEE-2015, here at BITS PILANI.
  • The teams would be provided a maximum time of 10 minutes for their respective presentations followed by a 5 minutes round of cross-questioning by our Judging panel.
  • The presentation should cover all the details of the solution, namely, the idea, the methodology, cost-effectiveness, process of implementation, total cost which would be incurred for implementing the project, its feasibility and sustainability.
  • Along with the report and presentation, the finalists may come up with a working model of their solution, which explains their concept better, and hence, enhances clarity. (The best use you make of this, the more credit you shall be awarded).
  • The Jury’s decision shall be deemed final and binding.

Implementation

The last phase will involve implementation of the solutions. Some projects (from those selected for the final round) will be carried out at the ground level, with help from the concerned NGOs in as many ways as possible. This might take the form of an internship based on the preferences of the organization and the students

Rules and guidelines

General Guidelines


  • The competition is open to all students and research scholars.
  • Teams of maximum 4 can participate in the event. The participants can be from different colleges.
  • All participants are required to register on the Apogee 2015 website www.bits-apogee.org , and register for DHITI.
  • The teams must submit their abstracts by 4th february, 2015.
  • Wild Card Round: The teams who have missed the deadline and would like to participate, have the chance to enter via the wildcard round. For this, participants must send in their final report to dhiti@bits-apogee.org by 6th March. If the project involves a model, a video showing the progress of the prototype also needs to be attached. Teams entering through the wildcard will not be provided mentorship. Their final report will be judged by a panel of expert from BITS.
  • Best 30 teams out of all the entries will be selected.The selected teams will be notified.
  • Decision of the judges shall be treated as final and binding on all.
  • At any point of time the latest information will be that which is on the website.However, registered participants will be informed through mail about any changes on the website.
  • The ideas presented by the teams should be original. Plagiarism of any kind will lead to disqualification of the team.
  • Certification – Shortlisted teams that present their solution in the final round in Apogee 2016 will receive a Participation. The best solutions will be appropriate certificates of distinction along with prizes.

Judging Criteria


  • Unique and innovative: They can be a modification of an already existing solution but should not be an exact replica of it.
  • Effective: They must provide better results than solutions which are already in use.
  • Cost-effective: For this the materials being used should be easily available and cheap.
  • Feasible: Keep in mind the target population/region. The ease of installation and the easy availability of the resources is also to be considered.
  • Sustainable: The solutions should be designed in such a way that they can be easily modified in future to adapt to the need and conditions of that time.
  • Social Impact: Should have a large social impact. It should affect the living conditions of a large number of people rather than only a few.
  • Replicable: Easily replicable so that they can be implemented over different regions rather than addressing a problem in a particular area only.

FAQs

1

How do I register for the event?

Registrations for Apogee and for Dhiti have started.The participant will have to first register for Apogee 2015 (visit http://www.bits-apogee.org ) and then register for Dhiti 2015.

2

Who can participate?

Any student, irrespective of his/her course or stream, who has registered for Apogee 2015 can participate in the event.

3

I am not an engineering student, can I still participate in the event?

Yes. Dhiti is meant for students of all disciplines, pursuing any degree. Graduates, Post-graduates, Diploma students, PhD holders etc.

4

I am unclear about the mentorship PHASE II. What am I supposed to do during mentorship?

Mentorship will be given to the participants who clear Phase I of the competition. Mentorship will be given in two forms: During the mentorship phase, the selected participants are expected to contact the concerned NGOs for in-depth knowledge about the problems faced by them. Similarly, for any technical guidance, they may contact their mentor (from BITS faculty). Participants can also contact through mails or direct visits to the NGOs. The contacts will be provided after the participants clear Phase I.

5

I have missed the deadline but would still like to participate. Can I still participate?

Yes. There is a wildcard entry round in which interested participants can send in their final report by 5th march 2015. If the project involves a model, a video showing the progress of the prototype also needs to be attached.

6

Is there any registration fee for the event?

No. The registration fees will be charged for Apogee only. There will not be separate registration fee for Dhiti

7

I have already presented this solution in another competition/ research paper, can I still present it here?

Yes. As long as there is no compulsion or binding on part of the participant from the concerned competition, the solution may be presented for Dhiti.

8

Does my project have to belong to categories mentioned, or they can be related to any field?

Your project can belong to any field. You just need to be sure about its feasibility and applicability.

9

Is taking part in Phase IV necessary?

It is not compulsory for the winners (or any other participant) to participate in Phase-4 (i.e. go to some place and get the idea implemented by yourself). The NGOs that are willing to implement the idea will do it themselves if you can't participate. But of course, your participation in Phase-4 is most welcome.
